paper user interfaces
A system of interfaces for paper-based electronic products

Home printed electronics is a promising direction of personal fabrication technology. Successful experiments in transforming home ink-jet printers to be used for tracing circuits onto paper, can suggest a future where a range of electronic objects will be produced directly, through an accessible and affordable process.

Drawing from analog electronics interfaces and digital touch-based gestures, a system of controllers and interactions for paper objects has been devised.

Considering electronics from a new production angle, PUI aims to blend the boundaries of the different fields involved into the conception of interfaces, where electronic functions are materialised through graphics and three dimensional shapes. Employing a versatile material, printable electronics allows interfaces to be shaped in semantic ways.

A series of lights - each representing a specific interface - exemplifies each interaction/behaviour pair of the proposed system. PUI is an exploration of the material. It’s a sandbox for design & development of printable and flexible electronic products.

①
Light turns on when paper corner is folded.
②
Light fades in when the white circle is slided on top of the black surface
③
When the paper solid stands on the small area, light is sharp and bright. When the paper solid lays in the big area, light is dim and warm.
④
Light brightness is controlled by touching the cone surface.
⑤
Light color is controlled by sliding a finger around paper solid surface.

Paper User Interfaces is a project by Leonardo Amico and Coralie Gourguechon developed for the exhibition Atelier Clerici, Milan 2015.
